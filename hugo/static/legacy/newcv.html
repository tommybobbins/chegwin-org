Work Experience

  • I am currently studying for a Ph.D. in the physical chemistry department of Nottingham University. My work involves laser photofragment spectroscopy and mass spectrometry of molecular ions. As part of the project, I have been involved in the design and development of a jet cooled cluster ion source.
  • During my Ph.D. I worked part time as a demonstrator in the second year physical chemistry laboratories and as a supervisor to two third year undergraduate dissertation projects.
  • My undergraduate vacation work was a student placement in the Resources Department of Sheffield Hallam University. My main responsibilities were relocation management and building surveying.

Relevant Skills

  • My studies rely upon the the day to day maintenance of mass spectrometers.
  • I have achieved a good understanding (both theoretically and experimentally) of vacuum techniques.
  • I have designed and built ion optics for a new jet cooled cluster ion source for our laboratory.
  • During my Ph.D. I have gained a thorough experience of data management and programming (Fortran and Pascal) working on a UNIX platform. I am also the system administrator for a DEC Alpha workstation and have a sound knowledge of internet protocol and use, including setting up my own web site.
  • I have a broad background in the physical sciences. The transfer from a physics degree to a chemistry-based Ph.D. taught me to adapt quickly to a new academic challenge and to educate myself with minimal supervision.
  • In the third year of my undergraduate studies, I completed the workshop engineering course at Newcastle University.
